# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to OctoAcme's project management documentation hub. This folder contains the end-to-end process guides that define how OctoAcme plans, executes, and continuously improves product delivery across all cross-functional teams. Whether you are a new team member looking to get up to speed or a seasoned contributor refreshing your knowledge, these documents provide the authoritative reference for how we work.

## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme's project management approach is built on five core principles: **customer-first prioritization**, **iterative delivery**, **clear ownership**, **data-informed decisions**, and **psychological safety**. Every project moves through a consistent lifecycle—Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Close & Retrospective—ensuring that work is properly scoped before it begins, delivered in small testable increments, and thoroughly reviewed before it ships. Each project has a named Project Manager and Product Lead who are accountable for driving the work from idea to outcome.

Effective collaboration across roles is central to our delivery model. Project Managers coordinate schedules, risks, and communications; Product Managers own the roadmap and acceptance criteria; Developers implement and test features; QA validates quality gates; and Stakeholders provide inputs and approvals. Teams stay aligned through daily standups, weekly delivery syncs between PM and Product, monthly stakeholder updates, and clear escalation paths for blockers or scope changes. This communication cadence keeps everyone informed without unnecessary overhead.

Quality assurance is embedded throughout the delivery lifecycle rather than treated as a final step. Pull request workflows, layered testing (unit, integration, end-to-end), and defined Definition of Done criteria ensure that only production-ready code is merged and deployed. CI/CD pipelines enforce quality gates automatically, and release checklists guide teams through safe deployment and post-release verification so that issues are caught early and rollback options remain available.

Risk management and continuous improvement close the loop on every project. A living Risk Register tracks identified risks, their likelihood, impact, and mitigations, with cross-team dependencies surfaced proactively. After each release, the team holds a structured retrospective to capture what went well, what to improve, and concrete action items that are tracked to completion. Metrics such as cycle time, defect rates, and customer satisfaction are monitored over time to drive evidence-based process improvements across the organization.
