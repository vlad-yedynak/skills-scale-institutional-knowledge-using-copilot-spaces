# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers design and validate user experiences and interfaces. They bridge the gap between end-user needs and engineering implementation, ensuring products are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity UI assets
- Conduct user research and usability testing to validate design decisions
- Translate user feedback and requirements into actionable design specifications
- Maintain and evolve the design system and component library
- Collaborate with developers to ensure accurate implementation of designs

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Reduce friction and increase user satisfaction with product interfaces
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and usability testing sessions
- Annotated design files and specification handoffs
- Sprint reviews to refine and validate user-facing features

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Works closely with Product Manager to understand user requirements, validate design concepts against product goals, and align on feature scope
- **Developers**: Provides developers with design assets, specifications, and clarification to ensure faithful implementation of UI components
- **Project Manager**: Participates in sprint reviews and planning sessions, providing design progress updates and flagging dependencies on engineering

---

## DevOps/Platform Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps/Platform Engineers maintain CI/CD pipelines, automation infrastructure, and the reliability, scalability, and security of project environments. They enable development teams to deliver software efficiently and safely.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure, environments, and monitoring systems
- Enforce security, compliance, and reliability standards across platforms
- Advise development and QA teams on deployment best practices
- Respond to infrastructure incidents and drive root cause analysis

### Goals
- Maximize deployment frequency while minimizing failure rates
- Ensure system reliability, scalability, and security for all environments
- Reduce manual toil through automation and tooling improvements

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure updates and incident postmortems
- Runbooks and platform documentation
- Async collaboration via issue trackers and deployment dashboards

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates with developers to ensure seamless deployments, troubleshoot environment issues, and integrate infrastructure requirements into feature work
- **Project Manager**: Integrates with Project Manager on release planning, communicating deployment windows, risks, and infrastructure readiness
- **QA (when present)**: Supports test environment management and ensures parity between staging and production environments

---

## Data Analyst/Scientist

### Role Summary
Data Analysts/Scientists define and implement metrics for product success, analyze user and system data to inform product direction, and develop dashboards that support evidence-based decision-making.

### Responsibilities
- Define, instrument, and track key product and business metrics
- Analyze user behavior, system performance, and business data
- Build and maintain dashboards and reporting pipelines
- Identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities from data
- Communicate findings and recommendations to stakeholders

### Goals
- Enable data-driven product and business decisions
- Ensure reliable, accessible, and trustworthy data and metrics
- Surface actionable insights that improve outcomes for users and the business

### Typical Communication
- Data reports and dashboard walkthroughs
- Ad hoc analysis requests and findings summaries
- Retrospective contributions with quantitative insights

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Supports Product Manager in measuring outcomes against success metrics and validating the impact of product changes
- **Developers**: Collaborates with developers on data instrumentation, event tracking, and assessing technical feasibility of data requirements
- **Project Manager**: Informs retrospectives and project reviews with actionable, data-backed insights to guide future planning

---

## Support/Customer Success Lead

### Role Summary
Support/Customer Success Leads capture user feedback, manage tickets, and surface recurrent issues to the product and engineering teams. They serve as the voice of the customer and ensure users are supported through product changes.

### Responsibilities
- Manage user support queues and escalate critical issues appropriately
- Identify and document recurring pain points and improvement opportunities
- Communicate product changes and release notes to users
- Collaborate with product and engineering on high-impact fixes and feature requests
- Validate that resolved issues meet user expectations before closure

### Goals
- Maximize user satisfaction and minimize support friction
- Ensure user pain points are heard and prioritized appropriately
- Support smooth pre- and post-release user communication

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket summaries and escalation reports
- User feedback digests shared with product and engineering
- Release communication and user-facing documentation

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates with Product Manager and stakeholders to prioritize high-impact fixes and features informed by real user feedback
- **Developers**: Provides developers with detailed, real-world bug reports and usage scenarios to improve diagnostic accuracy and resolution quality
- **Project Manager**: Partners with Project Manager to ensure support considerations are factored into release planning and post-release monitoring
